Sukhoi Su-57 Felon

Russia's premier fifth-generation stealth multirole fighter, engineered for air superiority and precision strike in the modern battlespace.

Redefining Air Dominance

The Sukhoi Su-57 (NATO reporting name: "Felon") is the culmination of decades of Russian aerospace innovation. Designed by the Sukhoi Design Bureau, it represents a leap into the fifth generation, combining stealth, supermanoeuvrability, supercruise, integrated avionics, and a large internal payload capacity.

Intended to succeed the venerable Su-27 and MiG-29 families, the Su-57 is a versatile platform capable of engaging air, ground, and maritime targets. Its design philosophy balances low observability with exceptional agility, making it a formidable opponent in any combat scenario. From deep-strike missions to defensive counter-air operations, the Felon is built to secure the skies.

Core Capabilities

Stealth & Aerodynamics

The Su-57 features a carefully shaped airframe and the use of composite materials to reduce its radar cross-section (RCS), particularly from the frontal aspect. Internal weapons bays maintain its stealth profile while carrying a formidable arsenal.

Supermanoeuvrability

Equipped with 3D thrust-vectoring nozzles, the Su-57 boasts extreme agility. It can perform post-stall manoeuvres like the Pugachev's Cobra and the flat spin, giving it a significant edge in within-visual-range combat.

Advanced Avionics

The core of the Su-57 is its integrated avionics suite. The Sh-121 multifunctional integrated radio electronic system (MIRES) includes multiple AESA radar arrays, providing 360-degree situational awareness and advanced electronic warfare capabilities.

Technical Specifications

Performance

  • Maximum Speed: Mach 2+ (2,140 km/h)
  • Supercruise: Mach 1.6
  • Range: 3,500 km (subsonic)
  • Service Ceiling: 20,000 m (66,000 ft)
  • G-limit: +9 g

Dimensions

  • Length: 20.1 m
  • Wingspan: 14.1 m
  • Height: 4.6 m
  • Wing Area: 78.8 m²

Armament

  • Guns: 1× 30 mm GSh-30-1 autocannon
  • Internal Bays: 4 (for missiles/bombs)
  • External Hardpoints: 6
  • Payload: Air-to-air, air-to-surface, anti-ship, and anti-radiation missiles.
